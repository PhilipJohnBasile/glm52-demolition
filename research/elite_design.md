## 1. THE ELITE CANON

### 1.1 Swiss / International Typographic Style

**Josef Müller-Brockmann** (1914–1996)
Signature principle: *The grid as moral act.* Information organized on a mathematical grid is not a stylistic choice — it is an ethical one. The grid forces the designer to be accountable: every element has a reason for its position. His 1966 *Grid Systems in Graphic Design* codified the modular column grid, the unit system, and the principle that white space is as active as printed matter. Posters for the Zurich Tonhalle: pure geometry, bold sans-serif, radical crop. Nothing decorative, everything load-bearing.

**Emil Ruder** (1914–1988)
Signature principle: *Typography is the discipline of organized silence.* Ruder taught at the Basel School of Design and codified Swiss typography in his 1967 *Typography: A Manual of Design*. He held that the counter-forms — the spaces inside and around letters — carry as much weight as the strokes. Flush-left/ragged-right, never justified. Rhythm across the page. Type as system, not decoration.

**Armin Hofmann** (b. 1920)
Signature principle: *Point, line, plane before everything else.* Hofmann's *Graphic Design Manual* (1965) begins not with layouts but with fundamental mark-making. He trained generations at Basel to see contrast, rhythm, and proportion as the raw material of communication. His Basel Theatre posters — a single photographic crop, one typeface, asymmetric tension — demonstrate that restraint is not poverty but precision.

**Jan Tschichold** (1902–1974)
Signature principle: *Asymmetry is the rhythmic expression of functional design.* In *Die Neue Typographie* (1928) Tschichold argued that symmetrical layouts impose decorative convention over communicative logic. Sans-serif type, flush-left setting, deliberate use of rules and space as structural elements, not ornament. Later pivoted to classical book typography (Penguin Books, 1947–49), proving elite design is not one style but mastery of underlying principles.

**Wim Crouwel** (1928–2019)
Signature principle: *The designer's ego must serve the system.* Co-founder of Total Design (Amsterdam, 1963), designed the visual identity of the Dutch PTT postal service — a systematic programme spanning stamps, forms, vehicles, and signage, all held together by a single grid logic. His 1967 typeface *New Alphabet* reduced letterforms to only horizontal and vertical strokes, embracing the cathode-ray tube as a design constraint rather than fighting it. Crouwel: "Design is not about decoration, it is about communication."

**Otl Aicher** (1922–1991)
Signature principle: *Total system design — every surface is a message.* Co-founder of the Ulm School of Design (HfG Ulm), Aicher built the 1972 Munich Olympics as a complete visual system: pictograms on a 45°/90° grid, a unified color palette, consistent Helvetica application across all wayfinding. For Lufthansa: melon yellow, lowercase Helvetica Bold, a redesigned crane — one coherent language across everything from boarding passes to aircraft livery.

**Alexey Brodovitch** (1898–1971)
Signature principle: *Astonish me — and use silence to do it.* Art director of *Harper's Bazaar* 1934–1958. Brodovitch treated the double-page spread as a single field of tension: full bleeds beside near-empty pages, Bodoni at monumental scale beside photographs that broke the frame. He commissioned Avedon, Penn, Cartier-Bresson. His demand to students: "If you see something you've seen before, don't press the shutter." Blank space as editorial voice.

### 1.2 American Modernism

**Paul Rand** (1914–1996)
Signature principle: *Reduce until only the idea remains.* Rand's logos for IBM, ABC, UPS, Westinghouse survive because they are not illustrations of companies — they are geometric propositions that happen to identify companies. His method: understand the problem → find the underlying visual metaphor → reduce until nothing superfluous remains. "Don't try to be original. Just try to be good." His IBM striped logo (1972) adds one layer of wit — the stripes — onto an otherwise irreducible letterform.

**Saul Bass** (1920–1996)
Signature principle: *Symbolize and summarize.* Bass collapsed the entire emotional content of a film into a single image: a fragmented arm and needle (*The Man with the Golden Arm*), a spiral of vertigo (*Vertigo*), a kinetic zigzag (*North by Northwest*). He proved that minimal graphic language could carry maximum psychological weight. His title sequences turned dead screen time into integrated cinema. Every element earns its place or it is cut.

**Milton Glaser** (1929–2020)
Signature principle: *Ornament is not crime — it is content, when it carries meaning.* Co-founder of Push Pin Studios (1954), Glaser was the great counter-voice to Modernist puritanism. His 1966 Bob Dylan poster — silhouette profile, Islamic-inspired jewel-hair derived from Duchamp — proved that historical reference and decoration could be as rigorous as a Swiss grid, if the idea is precise. The *I ❤ NY* logo (1976): three letters, one symbol, one typeface, zero wasted moves.

**Herb Lubalin** (1918–1981)
Signature principle: *The letter is an image.* Lubalin treated typography as illustration. For *Avant Garde* magazine (1968–72) he designed ligatures and geometric letter combinations that transformed the masthead into sculpture. He invented the concept of "typographics" — type arranged so that the formal relationships between letters carry meaning beyond their phonetic value. ITC Avant Garde Gothic emerged directly from his masthead explorations.

**Paula Scher** (b. 1948)
Signature principle: *Scale and weight as voice.* Scher's 1994 Public Theater identity used 19th-century wood-type at confrontational scale — letters of wildly varying size jammed together with deliberate tension, red and black, vertical stacking. Not a logo but a typographic attitude. Her large-scale painted maps (2006–present) operate on the same principle: information as texture, density as beauty. Partner at Pentagram since 1991.

**Michael Bierut** (b. 1957)
Signature principle: *Content generates form.* Partner at Pentagram, Bierut's method begins always with the meaning of the institution. The Yale School of Management identity (2011) uses a single typeface — Miller — in a systematic range of weights; no decoration is needed because the typographic system itself communicates authority and rigor. His rule: every design decision must be defensible in language.

### 1.3 German / Bauhaus Roots

**Dieter Rams** (b. 1932)
Signature principle: *Weniger, aber besser — less, but better.* Head of design at Braun 1961–1995, Rams produced objects of such formal clarity that they read as inevitable. His ten principles: good design is innovative, useful, aesthetic, understandable, unobtrusive, honest, long-lasting, thorough to the last detail, environmentally friendly, and — above all — as little as possible. The SK 4 record player (1956, with Hans Gugelot) was the direct ancestor of Apple's entire visual vocabulary. Jony Ive explicitly acknowledged this debt.

**Herbert Bayer** (1900–1985)
Signature principle: *One alphabet, one case, one weight — then stop.* Bauhaus master, Bayer designed the *Universal* typeface (1925): geometric sans-serif, lowercase only, no capital letters. The argument: capitals are historical convention, not functional necessity. His typophoto work for Bauhaus publications fused photography and typography into unified visual propositions, anticipating magazine design by a generation.

**Josef Albers** (1888–1976)
Signature principle: *Color has no absolute value — only relational value.* Bauhaus teacher, Yale professor, author of *Interaction of Color* (1963). Albers demonstrated through systematic exercises that the same color appears as two different colors depending on context. The implication for design: never specify a color in isolation; specify it in its relationship to every color it touches. His *Homage to the Square* series is applied color theory, not decoration.

### 1.4 British / Continental Wave

**Alan Fletcher** (1931–2006)
Signature principle: *Wit is rigor by other means.* Co-founder of Pentagram, Fletcher combined Swiss modernist discipline with a distinctly British sense of irony and wordplay. *The Art of Looking Sideways* (2001) is his extended argument that visual intelligence and verbal intelligence are the same faculty. His V&A logo (1989) — a 'V' and an ampersand forming the '&A' within a single letterform — is pure conceptual economy.

**Neville Brody** (b. 1957)
Signature principle: *Typography is image — it should behave like one.* Art director of *The Face* magazine 1981–1986, Brody used letterforms as structural and expressive elements independent of their communicative function. He broke the grid deliberately and with reason: grid-breaking signals cultural disruption. His 1984 *Industria* typeface — tall, geometric, forward-leaning — embodied the tension of post-punk London. Co-founder of FontShop (1989).

**Stefan Sagmeister** (b. 1962)
Signature principle: *Content must cost the designer something.* For the 1999 AIGA Detroit lecture poster, Sagmeister had all text carved into his own torso with an X-Acto knife. This was not shock tactic — it was literalization: design about pain should inflict pain in its making. His principle is that genuine emotional risk in the process produces genuine emotional resonance in the audience. Studio Sagmeister & Walsh.

**Jony Ive** (b. 1967)
Signature principle: *The best design disappears into use.* Chief Design Officer at Apple 1997–2019. Ive's hardware work (iMac G3, iPod, iPhone, MacBook Air) treated material and process as the language of design — the first translucent polycarbonate iMac communicated joy through material, not graphic applied to surface. iOS 7 (2013): flat design not as style but as the removal of metaphor — the interface recedes so content advances.

**Tibor Kalman** (1949–1999)
Signature principle: *Design without a position is decoration.* Founder of M&Co (New York), creative director of *Colors* magazine (Benetton, 1991–95). Kalman's M&Co operated on three commitments: heterogeneity (consistency through variation, not repetition), anti-slickness (deliberate vernacular imperfection as authenticity signal), and meaning over form. He said: "Designers are supposed to be dangerous. We're supposed to question assumptions and challenge the status quo."

### 1.5 Canonical Theorists (non-practitioners but load-bearing)

**Robert Bringhurst** (b. 1946)
*The Elements of Typographic Style* (1992) — the typographer's bible. Classical page proportions (2:3 ratio or golden section ~1:1.618), leading as breath between lines (120–145% of type size for text), letter-spacing only for all-caps or small caps, never for lowercase. The rule: "Typography exists to honor its content."

## 2. CHECKABLE ELITENESS CRITERIA

These are binary-checkable properties that separate elite from competent.
"Competent" = technically correct, conventionally assembled, no obvious errors.
"Elite" = every decision is a deliberate argument; form and content are inseparable.

### 2.1 Color

| Property | Competent | Elite |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Color space | Hex/RGB named colors | OKLCH (`oklch(L C H)`) — perceptually uniform, gamut-aware |
| Palette construction | Picked by eye or from a tool | Systematic: fixed L-step (e.g. 0.08) across a hue, 3–5 stops |
| Neutrals | `#333` `#666` `#eee` | OKLCH neutrals with micro-chroma (C ≈ 0.01–0.03) for warmth |
| Contrast | Passes WCAG AA by accident | Deliberately computed: text L ≥ 0.15 delta from bg L |
| Accent | 1–2 arbitrary brand colors | 1 accent, derived from same hue family as neutrals, intentional |
| Gradients | CSS `linear-gradient` with hex stops | None, or a single OKLCH gradient with consistent chroma |

### 2.2 Grid & Spacing

| Property | Competent | Elite |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Spacing unit | Arbitrary px values | 8px base unit; all spacing multiples of 8 (or 4 for tight contexts) |
| Layout grid | Flexbox centering | CSS Grid, explicit columns (e.g. `repeat(12, 1fr)`, `gap: 32px`) |
| Column count | Implicitly 1 or 2 | Named: 12-column, or a deliberate 3+9 / 4+8 split |
| Margins | `padding: 20px` | Generous: body max-width ~72ch for reading, ≥64px horizontal margin on desktop |
| Gutters | None or arbitrary | Named and consistent: 24px or 32px |
| Vertical rhythm | Line-height set once | All vertical distances are multiples of the leading unit |

### 2.3 Typography

| Property | Competent | Elite |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Type scale | Ad hoc sizes (14, 16, 22, 36px) | Modular scale: base × ratio^n (ratio = 1.25 Major Third or 1.333 Perfect Fourth) |
| Typeface | System font or Google Font default | Deliberate choice: named cut (e.g. Neue Haas Grotesk Display vs Text), weight as voice |
| Hierarchy | Bold + slightly larger | ≥3 distinct levels with different size, weight, and/or tracking |
| Line length | Uncontrolled | 45–75 characters (ch units in CSS) |
| Leading (line-height) | 1.5 default | 1.2–1.35 for display; 1.5–1.65 for body; never "inherit" |
| Tracking | Never touched | Slight negative tracking on large display (-0.02em); slight positive on all-caps small (+0.08em) |
| Widow/orphan control | None | `text-wrap: balance` or manual `max-width` on headings |
| Numerals | Default | Tabular lining numerals in tables (`font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums lining-nums`) |

### 2.4 Hierarchy & Composition

| Property | Competent | Elite |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Visual entry point | Unclear or multiple competing | One dominant element; eye path is designed, not accidental |
| Whitespace | Padding to avoid cramping | Whitespace as voice: sections breathe, negative space shapes positive |
| Alignment | Mixed left/center/right | Strict alignment system: one axis per section |
| Imagery | Stock photos, illustrative | Purposeful: cropped, sized, positioned as compositional element |
| Section rhythm | Each section independent | Consistent vertical rhythm creates read-through momentum |
| Restraint | Every feature used | Deliberate subtraction: what was removed is as important as what remains |

### 2.5 Anti-Patterns (Immediate Disqualifiers)

- **Bootstrap / Tailwind utility soup** — `.btn-primary`, `flex justify-between items-center`, `text-gray-600` — cookie-cutter spacing, pre-baked components with no authorial voice
- **Color named with hex from a picker** — `#3498db`, `#2ecc71`, `#e74c3c` — the Flat UI Colors palette is the signature of no decision
- **Box shadows on everything** — `box-shadow: 0 4px 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.1)` applied uniformly is a competence marker, not an elite one
- **Random border-radius** — `border-radius: 8px` or `50%` applied without system logic
- **Font-size: 14px body** — below comfortable reading threshold on modern displays
- **`margin: 0 auto` with `max-width: 1200px` and nothing else** — the generic container with no column thinking
- **Google Fonts `@import` with default weights only** — no variable font axis use, no optical size consideration
- **Centered headlines with left-body-text** — alignment schizophrenia
- **Repetition degeneration** — generated content that reuses the same phrase, pattern, or structural move >2× without variation; a sign the model is looping not composing

## 4. THE AUDIT

*Python pseudocode for gating eliteness. Run as a post-generation check before any output is accepted as SFT gold.*

```python
import re
from collections import Counter

# ── helpers ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

def extract_css(html: str) -> str:
    """Pull all <style> block content."""
    return "\n".join(re.findall(r"<style[^>]*>(.*?)</style>", html, re.DOTALL))

def extract_class_attrs(html: str) -> list[str]:
    """Return all class attribute values as flat token list."""
    raw = re.findall(r'class=["\']([^"\']+)["\']', html)
    return [tok for line in raw for tok in line.split()]

def ngrams(text: str, n: int) -> list[tuple]:
    words = text.lower().split()
    return list(zip(*[words[i:] for i in range(n)]))

# ── rule set ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

def audit(html: str) -> dict:
    css   = extract_css(html)
    klass = extract_class_attrs(html)
    fails = []

    # --- R1: OKLCH required ---
    if not re.search(r'\boklch\s*\(', css):
        fails.append("R1:FAIL — no oklch() found in CSS; hex/rgb palette is not elite")

    # --- R2: 8px grid — all spacing px values must be multiples of 4 ---
    spacing_vals = [int(m) for m in re.findall(r'(?:padding|margin|gap|top|left|right|bottom)\s*:\s*(\d+)px', css)]
    bad_spacing  = [v for v in spacing_vals if v % 4 != 0]
    if bad_spacing:
        fails.append(f"R2:FAIL — non-grid spacing values {bad_spacing}; must be multiples of 4px")

    # --- R3: type scale — at least 3 distinct font-size values ---
    sizes = set(re.findall(r'font-size\s*:\s*([^;]+);', css))
    if len(sizes) < 3:
        fails.append(f"R3:FAIL — only {len(sizes)} font-size(s); need ≥3 for a real type scale")

    # --- R4: reject Bootstrap / Tailwind tells ---
    FRAMEWORK_TELLS = {
        # Bootstrap
        "btn", "btn-primary", "btn-secondary", "col-md", "container-fluid",
        "navbar", "navbar-brand", "card-body", "form-control", "d-flex",
        # Tailwind utility classes (common patterns)
        "text-gray-600", "text-gray-700", "text-blue-500", "bg-white",
        "flex", "items-center", "justify-between", "rounded-lg", "shadow-md",
        "px-4", "py-2", "mt-4", "mb-4", "w-full", "h-full", "font-bold",
    }
    hits = [c for c in klass if c in FRAMEWORK_TELLS]
    if hits:
        fails.append(f"R4:FAIL — framework utility classes detected {hits[:5]}; bespoke CSS required")

    # --- R5: reject flat-ui hex palette signatures ---
    FLAT_UI_SIGS = ["#3498db", "#2ecc71", "#e74c3c", "#f39c12", "#9b59b6", "#1abc9c"]
    for sig in FLAT_UI_SIGS:
        if sig.lower() in html.lower():
            fails.append(f"R5:FAIL — flat-ui signature color {sig}; no authorial palette decision")

    # --- R6: reject body font-size 14px (too small) ---
    if re.search(r'body\s*\{[^}]*font-size\s*:\s*14px', css, re.DOTALL):
        fails.append("R6:FAIL — body font-size 14px; minimum is 16px for readable body text")

    # --- R7: heading line-height must be set (≤ 1.3 for display) ---
    # Heuristic: look for h1/h2 with line-height > 1.5 — over-leaded display
    for m in re.finditer(r'h[12][^{]*\{([^}]+)\}', css):
        block = m.group(1)
        lh    = re.search(r'line-height\s*:\s*([\d.]+)', block)
        if lh and float(lh.group(1)) > 1.4:
            fails.append(f"R7:WARN — display heading line-height {lh.group(1)} > 1.4; should be ≤1.3")

    # --- R8: tabular numerals in tables ---
    if "<table" in html and "tabular-nums" not in css:
        fails.append("R8:WARN — table present but font-variant-numeric:tabular-nums not set")

    # --- R9: repetition / degeneration guard ---
    # Trigram repeat: any trigram appearing > REPEAT_CAP times = looping
    REPEAT_CAP = 4
    text_only  = re.sub(r'<[^>]+>', ' ', html)  # strip tags
    trigrams   = ngrams(text_only, 3)
    counts     = Counter(trigrams)
    repeated   = [(g, c) for g, c in counts.items() if c > REPEAT_CAP
                  and not all(w in {"the","and","of","to","a","in","is","it","for"} for w in g)]
    if repeated:
        top = repeated[:3]
        fails.append(f"R9:FAIL — degeneration detected; top repeated trigrams {top}")

    # --- R10: semantic HTML — require at least one landmark element ---
    LANDMARKS = ["<main", "<nav", "<header", "<footer", "<article", "<section", "<aside"]
    if not any(lm in html for lm in LANDMARKS):
        fails.append("R10:FAIL — no semantic landmark elements; use <main>, <article>, etc.")

    # ── verdict ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    return {
        "pass":   len(fails) == 0,
        "score":  max(0, 10 - len(fails)),
        "fails":  fails,
        "status": "ELITE" if len(fails) == 0 else ("REVIEW" if len(fails) <= 2 else "REJECT"),
    }


# ── usage ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

#   ELITE  (0 fails)  → include as positive gold
#   REVIEW (1-2 fails)→ human review before inclusion
#   REJECT (3+ fails) → discard; do not include in training set
```

### 4.1 Audit Rules Summary

| Rule | Check | Severity |
|------|-------|----------|
| R1 | `oklch()` present in CSS | FAIL |
| R2 | All spacing px-values divisible by 4 | FAIL |
| R3 | ≥3 distinct `font-size` values (type scale) | FAIL |
| R4 | No Bootstrap/Tailwind class signatures | FAIL |
| R5 | No flat-UI hex signature colors | FAIL |
| R6 | Body font-size ≥ 16px | FAIL |
| R7 | Display heading `line-height` ≤ 1.4 | WARN |
| R8 | Tables use `tabular-nums lining-nums` | WARN |
| R9 | No trigram repeats > 4× (degeneration guard) | FAIL |
| R10 | Semantic landmark HTML element present | FAIL |

**SFT gate:** FAIL rules are hard gates — any single FAIL = REJECT from training corpus. WARN rules are soft — two WARNs = REVIEW, requiring human sign-off before inclusion.
